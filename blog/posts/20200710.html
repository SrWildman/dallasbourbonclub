Review: Tiger Juice & Partagas Series D #4

This week, we've got a special treat. Not only do we have a member review, we've got the first ever Cigar & Whiskey pairing review. Brian McMeans put together this pairing and review for us earlier this week and I am excited to share it with you. Brian; take it away!

Couple of quick notes: I opened my Tiger Juice on Day 1 and enjoyed it, but I was not wowed by any means. I have always enjoyed the PSD4 and have never been let down by this smoke (probably smoked over 50 of these). I got a fresh box of PSD4’s on the day of the review. The box code was from January 2020, so this is the least aged PSD4 that I have ever had.

Review: The PSD4 is a robusto sized cigar of Cuban origin and should be on the medium flavor / strength profile. The freshness of this cigar was evident from the barnyard and cedar aromas pre-light, but the leather and earthiness replaced that smell quickly upon toasting and lighting the cigar. First couple of draws of smoke were full of leather and cedar, I was also getting a bit of white pepper.

First pour of Tiger Juice was about 3 ounces, I let the alcohol vapors dissipate while prepping my smoke. After a quick swirl of the Tiger Juice (Pete and Michael Langston are right about legs for days), I’m off to the races!

I took my time with the first pour and half of the PSD4. I have really begin trying to focus on the balance in Cigar / Whiskey pairing since the Zoom Happy Hour featuring Jake. I think these two go together perfectly bringing out some of the flavors that I adore in both whiskey and cigars. Leather and Woodiness are prevalent in this pairing with a hint of brown sugar (like homemade cookies). Cigar’s construction was flawless with a smooth draw and just the right amount of smoke; the bite on the Tiger Juice was just enough to let me take my time.

At the halfway point, I needed a refill and decided to try a cube of ice in the second pour. Great call as both the TJ and the PSD4 opened up a bit. I was getting a bit more of the sweetness (Caramel, Brown Sugar, Vanilla, Creaminess) as these two hit their stride, I really wanted a homemade cookie at this point. The second half of the cigar seemed to fly by with the final third adding an additional jolt of nicotine and white pepper.

Overall: What a great pairing! The flavors and strength of both, really complement each other. The only flavor that seemed to be washed out was the oak of the TJ (replaced with a sweet cedar). The PSD4 is definitely a medium to strong smoke at this age, and the flavors are pretty intense (should age well if I don’t smoke them all quickly). I can’t wait to try the Tiger Juice with an aged PSD4!
